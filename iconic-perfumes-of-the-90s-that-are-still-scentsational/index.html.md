# Iconic Perfumes of the 90s That Are Still Scentsational Today ...

**Iconic perfumes** of the '90s have a unique, almost magical ability to transport us back to an era of dial-up internet, butterfly clips, and unforgettable fashion choices. Now, in 2024, as we’re living in the future and yet drowning in nostalgia, it’s intriguing-and somewhat humorous-to see that these “_throwback scents_” have retained their original charm. But why, oh why, have these **iconic perfumes** stood the test of time, unlike my _Tamagotchi_, which didn’t even make it through '98?

Allow me to share a personal story (we all love those, don’t we?). Picture this: I’m a college student, the year is 1996, and my room is an absolute shrine to ‘90s culture. Posters of the Backstreet Boys cover every inch of wall space, there’s a CD tower stacked with _boyband albums_, and, of course, the pièce de résistance-my prized collection of **iconic perfumes** sitting atop my dresser. Remember how everyone had a _signature scent_ back then? Mine was Calvin Klein's CK One, and I’m convinced that spritzing it on every morning gave me superpowers… or at least made me believe I was cool while stressing over final exams.

Fast forward to today, and guess what? That same bottle of CK One-or perhaps its more modern iteration-is still a hot commodity. Turns out, I’m not the only one who appreciates a good scent trip down memory lane. Our Table of Contents will take you through a variety of stages, from ["The Cultural Impact of 90's Scents"](#1) to ["Why the Notes Matter: A Deep Dive into the Ingredients"](#3).

So, what’s the secret sauce? Well, unlike other fads, **iconic perfumes** encapsulate the essence of an era-they're bottled memories! Also, brands have cleverly kept up with the times, reinventing these scents while staying true to their original allure.

Let’s not forget the _advertising icons_ of the '90s. You know who I’m talking about-those larger-than-life celebrities and supermodels who made you believe that just one spray could make you equally glamorous. Plus, there was something rebelliously chic about these ["Fragrances That Defined a Generation"](#2), whether it was grunge-inspired or squeaky clean.

In this article, we’ll explore why these fragrances remain popular and how they continue to influence the market today. We’ll dive into the _aromatic notes_, discuss the _fashion trends_ they complemented, and uncover the marketing genius behind them. So buckle up, because we’re about to journey back in time-with our noses leading the way!

## 1. Obsession

![Dark Obsession, perfume, nectar, cosmetics, skin,](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/hc/wt/oxo3jxt5579d814a51f7c721673478_600x730.jpg)

Get it here: [fragrancenet.com](http://www.fragrancenet.com?ref=allwomenstalk.com)
Obsession by Calvin Klein is undoubtedly one of the most iconic scents of the '90s. Certainly, not one for the more subtle perfume lover, Obsession’s silky amber, and oriental notes was a seriously big hitter back in the day. What made it so great was that it was one of the first major unisex perfumes, which meant that you could choose a partner based on your similar scent preferences!

## 2. CK One

![Calvin Klein, distilled beverage, alcoholic beverage, drink, glass bottle,](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/qs/y1/k6llw5yp579d8167631bc530933570_600x548.jpg)

Get it here: [sephora.com](http://www.sephora.com?ref=allwomenstalk.com)
When you think about perfumes in the '90s, one of the scents that immediately comes to mind is CK One. A fragrance made even more famous by the strong connection and partnership with '90s supermodel and constant tabloid figure Kate Moss, CK One became synonymous with the cool, grungy, waif-like style of the decade, but has since gone on to take on a more timeless reputation.

## 3. Trésor

![perfume, distilled beverage, drink, cosmetics, whisky,](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/lq/hw/u1feuc8i579d818e3818c540656734_500x352.jpg)

Get it here: [fragrancenet.com](http://www.fragrancenet.com?ref=allwomenstalk.com)
Trésor by Lancôme was a sensual amber smelling scent that was super popular in the '90s. It had a bit of a reputation for being a more sophisticated, mature scent that girls would spritz on for special occasions. Trésor was the perfect segue fragrance for young girls who wanted something a bit more mature to move on from their quirky, fruity scents.

## 4. Anaïs Anaïs

![Cacharel, perfume, flower, plant, rosa rubiginosa,](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/u8/bf/o69zpb4u579d81c0cc586599999544_500x497.jpg)

Get it here: [fragrancenet.com](http://www.fragrancenet.com?ref=allwomenstalk.com)
Anaïs Anaïs by Cacharel is another fragrance that will take you straight back to your teen years. With its pleasant white floral notes, the perfume was the perfect mixture of lush fragrance without being too overpowering - an ideal choice for sprucing up at school or spritzing for a weekend outing with your friends. And the best part is that it is still as appealing now as it was twenty years ago!

## 5. Angel

![Thierry Mugler, perfume, diagram, presentation, document,](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/dt/pn/czqv17a1579d820ee2efb952571316_570x531.jpg)

Get it here: [sephora.com](http://www.sephora.com?ref=allwomenstalk.com)
Angel by Thierry Mugler is immediately identifiable by smell, perhaps more so than any of the other perfumes on this list. The fragrance can be described as patchouli meets cotton candy, and it was definitely a case of love it or hate it back in the day! One of those scents that have just as enjoyable a bottle as it did a fragrance, the cool blue star was a permanent fixture on many a dressing table!

## 6. JPG Classique

![perfume, glass bottle, lotion, atmospheric phenomenon, cosmetics,](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/ra/de/s7o30dmq579d824282e71301513496_600x585.jpg)

Get it here: [houseofbeautyworld.com](https://www.houseofbeautyworld.com/)
Another '90s perfume situation where the bottle is equally as famous as the fragrance itself, perhaps even more so! Jean Paul Gaultier’s Classique was the epitome of cool during the decade, evoking images of Madonna in her glory years and possessing a wonderfully moreish musky, warm, almost vanilla-like scent. Favored by many of the biggest celebrities of the time, Classique really was sophistication in a bottle.

## 7. Sunflowers

![nectar, produce, perfume, cosmetics, lotion,](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/jf/jq/jm6erckg579d828d472f5544075526_600x600.jpg)

Get it here: [ice.com](https://www.ice.com?ref=allwomenstalk.com)
For some reason, whenever I think about images and objects of the '90s, sunflowers always come to mind. This could have something to do with the insanely popular perfume by Elizabeth Arden that was a worldwide favorite during the decade. Launched in 1993, the scent became one of the go-to choices for teens everywhere. How about we try to relive our own best years and bring it back once and for all?

I still reach for Sunflowers on the occasion I want something light and floral. Which of these did you wear? Are you wearing it still?
